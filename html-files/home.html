First Things First

OK, so you want a pet parrot, which is cool and all, but you're going tohave to know how to take care of your feather baby. First things first, there is no such thing as a starter bird. All parrots need tons of love and attention and all parrots have personalized likes and dislikes. However, despite this, there are birds with more information and tools tht you can use to care for them as opposed to more rare species, such as a budgie or cockatiel. Even so, they all need ample room to fly around and lots of toys and trinkets to keep their curious minds entertained. Any and all items that you own are now theirs, which means that they're going to want to destroy them. This means that you must "birdproof" all rooms that they have access to so that they wont get into anything harmful. They should have clean food and water at all times and their cage should be kept at least fairly clean. However, when you clean their cage, DO NOT use chemical cleaners or aerosol sprays of any kind! Their lungs are significantly more sensitive than ours and it WILL kill them! The same thing goes for teflon and non-stick pans, just don't use them.
